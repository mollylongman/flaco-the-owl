In Late February, Flaco began exploring and finding his home in the North Woods towards the top of the park. He was seen first on February 24th near the Huddlestone Arch.

He liked this area, and stayed around the North Woods and Huddlestone Arch for most of March as well. It's more seculuded up there than the South end of the park, especially in the Loch off the paved paths. But it's also close enough to flat baseballfields, which are prime spots for him to spot and catch rats for dinner.

He also was spotted frequently in this area in April, though he explored the park some as the weather warmed.

In May, he was also seen frequently in the North Woods, often in a Tulip poplar tree.

Towards the end of May, though, he began being mostly spotted by the Compost Heap around 105th and East Drive. The heap attracts rats, and so it was a good place for him to swoop down and catch his prey.

In June, he was mostly spotted by this compost area as well.
